### Overview

CTI Butler supports STIX bundle export so teams can move structured CTI objects and their relationships into the rest of the security stack.

This matters when the goal is not just to look something up, but to reuse the result in another system, archive it for later analysis, or pass it into an automation workflow that already expects STIX 2.1 content.

### Why Teams Use It

STIX bundle export gives teams a clean handoff point between CTI Butler and downstream tooling.

Instead of manually copying framework details into another platform, users can export the underlying structured objects in a format that is already familiar across many CTI and graph-oriented workflows.

### Example Use Cases

- Exporting ATT&CK, CAPEC, CWE, or related objects into internal tooling
- Passing structured reference intelligence into TIP, OpenCTI, or graph analysis workflows
- Preserving research context in a portable, machine-readable format

### Interoperability

STIX bundle export reinforces CTI Butler's role as a CTI context and interoperability layer.

It helps teams move from retrieval to operational use without losing the structure that makes the intelligence reusable.
