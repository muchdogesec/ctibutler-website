### Overview

Integrations pages explain how CTI Butler connects to other systems and workflows.

These pages focus on interoperability: what the integration is, why teams use it, and how it fits into wider CTI operations.

CTI Butler is most useful when the context it exposes can move cleanly into the rest of your stack. That is why the main integration paths are treated as a first-class part of the site architecture rather than buried inside general product copy.

That matters because CTI Butler is deliberately positioned as a context, retrieval, and interoperability layer. The value is not only in being able to view the data in CTI Butler itself, but in being able to export, sync, query, and operationalise that structured CTI in the systems that sit around it.
